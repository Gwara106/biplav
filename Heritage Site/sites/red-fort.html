Red Fort

A Symbol of Mughal Glory

Quick Information

  • Built: 1639-1648
  • Built by: Emperor Shah Jahan
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site: Yes (2007)
  • Opening Hours: Sunrise to Sunset (Closed on Mondays)
  • Entry Fee: ₹35 (Indians), ₹500 (Foreigners)

History

The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, was built in the 17th century by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, the same emperor who built the famous Taj Mahal. The fort served as the main residence of Mughal Emperors for nearly 200 years, until 1857.

Architecture

The fort's massive enclosing walls of red sandstone rise to a height of 33 meters (108 ft). The fort complex houses several museums and its architectural features showcase a blend of Persian, Timurid, and Indian traditions. Notable structures within include:

  • Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience)
  • Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience)
  • Rang Mahal (Palace of Colors)
  • Moti Masjid (Pearl Mosque)

Historical Significance

The Red Fort has witnessed numerous historical events and remains a powerful symbol of India's sovereignty. Every year on August 15th, the Prime Minister of India hoists the national flag at the fort's main gate to celebrate India's independence.

Conservation

The Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) maintains the fort as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Recent conservation efforts have focused on preserving the fort's original features while making it more accessible to visitors.
